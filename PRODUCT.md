# Product

## Register

product

## Users

RASEEN serves three primary user groups across the same shared system: platform admins who configure organizations, frameworks, monitoring coverage, and audit sessions; auditors who review evidence, compare submissions, and publish decisions; and client teams who prepare evidence, respond to revision requests, and track audit progress. All three groups work in high-consequence, detail-heavy workflows where they need confidence, clarity, and fast scanning more than novelty.

## Product Purpose

RASEEN turns cloud governance and compliance work into a structured product workflow instead of a fragmented mix of spreadsheets, email threads, screenshots, and scattered document review. The product exists to help teams run audit sessions, collect evidence, evaluate configuration-backed controls, and keep a trustworthy record of decisions and progress across admin, auditor, and client surfaces. Success looks like users moving through dense operational screens without hesitation, understanding status at a glance, and trusting that the system is consistent enough to support larger redesigns without losing audit rigor.

## Brand Personality

Serious, trusted, precise.

The voice should feel enterprise-grade without becoming stiff or intimidating. The interface should communicate operational control, calm judgment, and modern product quality. Admins should feel in control, auditors should feel supported by structure rather than buried in it, and client teams should feel guided instead of judged.

## Anti-references

- Do not let the product devolve into generic shadcn SaaS, with interchangeable neutral cards and no point of view.
- Do not let the product feel like cold audit bureaucracy, where density turns into intimidation and every screen feels punitive.
- Do not let the product drift into consumer-app playful, with casual visual language, cheerful color behavior, or lightweight interaction patterns that weaken trust.

## Design Principles

- **Trust through clarity.** Status, ownership, progress, and next actions should be obvious without extra interpretation.
- **One system across three roles.** Admin, auditor, and client surfaces may differ in density and priority, but they should feel like one product with one component language.
- **Distinctive restraint.** The system should become more recognizable and premium without relying on loud gradients, decorative chrome, or trend-following enterprise theater.
- **Operational density, not visual clutter.** Dense workflows are acceptable and often necessary, but hierarchy, spacing, and component states must keep them legible.
- **Accessibility is part of trust.** Strong contrast, keyboard-visible focus, dark-mode parity, and reduced-motion awareness are product requirements, not polish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The default accessibility bar is WCAG AA. Dark mode must remain a first-class surface, not a secondary theme. The current product posture is desktop-first for admin, auditor, and client workflows, but responsive behavior should still preserve readability and task completion on smaller screens. All core components should ship with visible focus states, keyboard support, legible dense layouts, semantic status meaning that does not rely on color alone, and reduced-motion-safe behavior.
